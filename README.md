The primary objective was to visualize the intersection of traditional brick-and-mortar sales with growing digital trends. The dashboard provides an interactive interface to monitor high-level KPIs and granular performance data across Australia’s states and territories.

Key Metrics and Performance Insights

    1) Revenue Scale: The dashboard reports Total Sales of $18,53,42 (represented as a high-level KPI) with an Average Sales value of $411.41 per transaction.
    
    2) Dominant Category: "Gaming, Tech & Audio" emerged as the top-performing product category, signaling strong consumer demand in the electronics sector.
    
    3) Store vs. E-Retail Split: Physical BIGW STORE locations continue to drive the majority of revenue at 72%, while the BIGW E-RETAIL channel has secured a significant 28% market share of total sales.
    
    4) Top Performance Hubs: Frankston was identified as the top-performing suburb, and Jarrah Walker was highlighted as the top-performing manager based on sales volume.

Geographic and Category Trends

    1) State-Level Performance: Analysis of "Sales by State" shows that New South Wales (NSW) and Victoria (VIC) are the primary revenue drivers. Interestingly, the data visualizes the ratio of store-to-online sales for each state, showing how digital adoption varies by region.
    
    2) Category Diversification: Beyond the tech sector, categories such as "Toys & Books" and "Health & Beauty" show consistent sales volume, which can be seen in the "Sales by Category" bar chart.
    
    3) Managerial Excellence: The "Sales by Manager" chart provides a leaderboard of staff performance, with leaders like Arjun Sharma and Bao Hoang following closely behind the top manager.
